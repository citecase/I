| 3 | Executive Engineer Irrigation Division  .v. Secretary Malampuzha Irrigation Thozhilali Union INTUC and others Issues for consideration (1)     Whether the Management, having admitted that the workmen were engaged for work in its establishment, discharged the burden of proving that there was no employer–employee relationship? (2)     Whether the continuous engagement of workmen in permanent and essential duties while denying them regular status constitutes an unfair labour practice under the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947\. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 (Central Act 14 of 1947)—Employer–Employee Relationship—Burden of proof—Once the Management admits that the workmen were engaged for work in the establishment belonging to it, the burden shifts to the Management to establish that the workmen were not its employees—Failure to produce evidence of contractual engagement, including contract documents, contractor details, or proof of wage payment through third parties, coupled with evidence of direct supervision and control by the Management, establishes a direct employer–employee relationship. | 9 | Canara Bank  .v. Rajendran C and another Issue for Consideration Whether the delay in raising an industrial dispute would result in dismissal of the dispute as a stale claim ?  Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 (Central Act 14 of 1947)—Sections 2(oo), 2(s), 10, 11A, 25F and 25FFF—Deposit Collectors are Workmen within the meaning of Section 2(s)—Delay  of 8 years in raising the dispute is not fatal to the claim and does not make the claim stale, as Management has not pleaded or proved any prejudice on account of delay—Even if the service of the Workman came to an end on account of expiry of contract or by discontinuance of the Deposit Scheme in which Workman was working, it amounts to termination of service—Closure of a deposit scheme by the Management would be covered by closing down of the undertaking within the meaning of Section 25-FFF and Workman is entitled to get notice and compensation under Section 25F—Management could not be ordered to pay back-wages for the period of delay in raising the dispute caused by the Workman—Limitation Act, 1963 (Central Act 36 of 1963\) —Article 137\.   Judge:Mr. Justice M. A. Abdul Hakhim||Judgment Date:02-12-2025 |
| 10 | Abdul Azeez  .v. Union of India and others Issue for Consideration Whether the Bank has the right or authority to freeze the account of its Account Holders on the basis of suspicion with respect to the transactions in the account by itself in the absence of any requisition to do so from a law enforcement agency?  Constitution of India—Article 226—Court conferred the Banks with the power to freeze the operations in the suspicious accounts for a reasonable period, without prior notice to the Account Holders—Debit freezing can be done by the Bank only for a reasonable period of three months, and if no action is taken by the law-enforcing agencies within the said period of three months, the Bank has to necessarily release the debit freezing—Court issued guidelines for freezing the operations in the account when the Bank entertains suspicion about transactions in the account—Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002 (Central Act 15 of 2003)—Section 12\.   Judge:Mr. Justice M. A. Abdul Hakhim||Judgment Date:19-11-2025 |
